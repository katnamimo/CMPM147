I really enjoyed this experiment. There was a lot of free creative reign and many ways to approach different problems. I'm hoping to really be back on track and less stressed soon.

Relation to inspiring image:

  • Color: The sky gradient and underside of clouds were picked with a dropper tool from the inspiration image using the website https://redketchup.io/color-picker.
  • Primitives: The clouds are rendered with clusters of varying ellipsis, and the stars are drawn with rect() primitives.
  • Polygons: The moon is drawn with beginShape() and endShape(), utilizing bezierVertex().
  • Composition: Different random seed values lead to differently shaped clouds and star positions.
  • Life: The clouds slowly drift and wrap across the scene to eliminate the need for constant generation of clouds. The screen may be clicked to spawn a 'shooting star'.
